# Pattern card — Bank Office - Phase 1
- **Source:** AnyLogic example (basicmodels) — distilled, original wording
- **Paradigm:** DES
- **Problem it solves:** Models a single-queue, multi-teller bank where customers arrive at a constant rate, wait in a bounded queue, and are served at a teller before leaving.

## Block chain
Customers are generated by a **Source** at a fixed arrival rate of 0.3 per minute. They enter a **Queue** (capacity capped at 15) to wait for an available teller. A **Delay** block represents the teller service time, drawn from a triangular distribution. Served customers exit through a **Sink**.

## Resources
No explicit ResourcePool; teller capacity is embedded in the Delay block. The Queue holds up to 15 waiting customers before new arrivals are blocked or lost.

## Key settings worth copying
- Arrival rate: 0.3 entities/minute (Poisson-equivalent constant rate via Source)
- Service time distribution: `triangular(0.8, 1.5, 3.5)` minutes (min, mode, max)
- Queue capacity: 15 entities
- Time unit: Minutes
- Statistics collection forced on the Queue block (`forceStatisticsCollection = true`)

## KPIs instrumented
- Queue length (waiting customers)
- Wait time in queue
- Teller utilisation (via Delay block statistics)
- Throughput (entities reaching the Sink)

## Reusable idea
Force statistics collection on the Queue even when it is rarely full — this gives accurate mean wait-time data across all runs without needing a separate data-collection block, which is the simplest way to instrument a bottleneck in a Phase-1 / baseline DES model.
