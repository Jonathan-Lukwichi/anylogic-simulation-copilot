# Pattern card — Bank Office - Phase 2
- **Source:** AnyLogic example (basicmodels) — distilled, original wording
- **Paradigm:** DES
- **Problem it solves:** Models customer flow through a single ATM with a waiting queue, demonstrating queue-plus-server dynamics with a triangular service time and animated 3D environment.

## Block chain
Customers are generated by a **Source** block at a configurable arrival rate, then routed into a **Queue** (capacity 15) where they wait for the ATM to become free. A **Delay** block representing the ATM performs the actual transaction. Customers exit through a **Sink** block once served.

## Resources
No explicit ResourcePool; the ATM acts as an implicit single-server resource captured by the Delay block's capacity (1 server). Queue capacity is set to 15 customers.

## Key settings worth copying
- **Service time:** `triangular(0.8, 1.5, 3.5)` minutes — a triangular distribution with minimum 0.8, most-likely 1.5, and maximum 3.5 minutes, ideal when you have expert estimates but no historical data.
- **Arrival:** rate-based Source using a `rateSchedule` or `rateExpression` parameter, making it easy to switch between constant and time-varying arrival rates.
- **Time unit:** Minutes throughout the model.
- **Queue capacity:** 15 (finite, mirroring a physical waiting area constraint).
- **3D animation:** ATM node color flips green/red depending on whether the Delay block is occupied (`ATM.size() > 0 ? red : green`), giving instant visual feedback on utilisation.

## KPIs instrumented
Queue length and wait time tracked implicitly via the Queue block's built-in statistics; ATM occupancy visible through the colour-coded 3D node. The model is positioned as a stepping-stone ("Phase 2"), implying progressive addition of KPI charts in later phases.

## Reusable idea
Using a **triangular distribution** for service time is the go-to trick when you lack data but can ask a subject-matter expert for a best case, most-likely case, and worst case — the three parameters map directly to those answers. Pair it with a finite-capacity Queue to enforce a physical space limit and expose blocking behaviour without needing a full resource-pool setup.
