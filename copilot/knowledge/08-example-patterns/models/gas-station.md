# Pattern card — Gas Station
- **Source:** AnyLogic example (models) — distilled, original wording
- **Paradigm:** Hybrid (DES + Fluid)
- **Problem it solves:** Simulate a fuel station where cars queue for dispensers while underground tanks drain continuously, requiring both discrete car flow and continuous fluid dynamics in one model.

## Block chain

Cars are generated by a **CarSource** and routed via a **CarMoveTo** block toward the station entrance. They then enter a **Seize** block that claims one of the four fuel dispensers from a shared resource pool (`fuelDispensersPool`). After seizing a dispenser, a **SelectOutput5** block routes the car to the correct pump island based on requested fuel type (gas 92, gas 95, or diesel). The car occupies the dispenser while fuel flows from the relevant underground **Tank** through a **FluidSplit4** splitter to the individual **FuelDispenser** fluid outlet. Once fuelling is complete a **Release** block frees the dispenser resource, and a second **CarMoveTo** drives the car off-site.

On the fluid side, three buried Tank objects (one per fuel grade) drain at up to 10 L/s each. A FluidSplit4 per tank distributes flow to the dispenser nozzles in parallel. Tank levels can be watched in real time; when a tank empties, connected dispensers of that grade are dynamically blocked — demonstrating live reconfiguration of the fluid network.

## Resources

- **fuelDispensersPool** — 4 FuelDispenser resource units (one per physical pump island), shared across all fuel types
- **Tanks** — 3 × underground Tank objects (gas92, gas95, diesel), capacity 30 m³ each, initial fill 3.5 m³
- **Flow rate limit** — 10 L/s max out-rate per tank

## Key settings worth copying

- Time unit: **Minute**
- Tank capacity: 30 m³; initial amount: 3.5 m³ (pre-filled at start)
- Tank `limitRateOut = true`, `maxRateOut = 10 L/s`
- Three fuel grades encoded as batch-type labels (GAS92, GAS95, DIESEL) set on Tank `initialBatch`
- SelectOutput5 routes on a car attribute (requested fuel type) — pattern for multi-product queuing
- `carSeizedDispenser(car, dispenser)` helper function verifies car-to-dispenser pairing via `resourceUnitOfPool` — reusable guard for selective release

## KPIs instrumented

- Dispenser utilisation (fraction of time each FuelDispenser is occupied)
- Tank level over time (fluid content chart per grade)
- Car throughput (cars served per hour)
- Queue length at station entry (Seize block statistics)

## Reusable idea

Combine a discrete resource pool (Seize/Release on dispenser units) with a continuous Fluid Library tank so that the same simulation simultaneously tracks customer waiting time AND physical inventory depletion — neither library alone can do both, but linking them through a shared resource-unit reference gives a clean hybrid model without custom Java glue code.
