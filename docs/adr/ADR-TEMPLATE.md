## Context
What is the situation and the forces at play? Write only enough that someone with no
prior context understands why a decision was even needed.

## Decision
The choice we are making, stated plainly.

## Consequences
What becomes easier, what becomes harder, and what we are now committed to. Note the
trade-off honestly — an ADR exists precisely because there was one.

---
Only write an ADR when the decision is (1) hard to reverse, (2) surprising without
context, and (3) the result of a real trade-off. A swap between interchangeable
libraries does not need one; a deletion-cascade rule or a persistence choice does.
